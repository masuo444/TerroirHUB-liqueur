Best Japanese Liqueurs 2026: 15 Must-Try Bottles

Japan produces some of the world's most distinctive liqueurs — from centuries-old umeshu traditions to cutting-edge matcha and sakura expressions. Here are 15 bottles that define the category in 2026.

Umeshu (Plum Liqueur)

  1. The CHOYA AGED 3 YEARS — A benchmark aged umeshu with layered depth. 3-year maturation with Nanko-ume plums.
  2. Baika Hyakunen Umeshu (Meiri Shurui) — Grand-prize winner at the National Umeshu Competition. Brandy-aged, complex.
  3. Kishu Umeshu (Nakano BC) — Pure Wakayama character. Clean, elegant, the quintessential Nanko-ume umeshu.
  4. Aragoshi Umeshu (Umenoyado Shuzo) — Sake-based with real plum pulp. Luxurious and textured.
  5. Hachikaizan no Umeshu (Hakkaisan) — From the renowned sake brewery. Refined and balanced.

Yuzu Liqueur

  1. Suntory Kanade Yuzu — Elegant yuzu character, globally available. Excellent as a soda highball.
  2. Tsukasa Botan Yuzushu (Kochi) — Uses Kochi's legendary yuzu. Bright, authentic citrus.

Peach and Fruit Liqueurs

  1. Aragoshi Momo (Umenoyado Shuzo) — White peach pulp in every sip. A dessert in a glass.
  2. Aragoshi Mikan (Umenoyado Shuzo) — Satsuma mandarin pulp. Sweet, nostalgic, irresistible.
  3. Tochiotome Ichigoshu — Made with Tochigi's famous strawberry cultivar.

Matcha, Sakura & Other

  1. Suntory Kanade Matcha — Uji matcha character, superb with milk or over ice cream.
  2. Suntory Kanade Sakura — Cherry-blossom elegance. The perfect spring aperitif.
  3. Suntory Kanade Hakuto — White peach by Suntory, refined and fragrant.
  4. Seifuku Shuzo Awamori Umeshu (Okinawa) — Umeshu on an awamori base — tropical depth.
  5. Hokkaido Haskap Liqueur — Wild berry from the north. Tart, vibrant, unique.

How to Choose

Consider the base spirit (white liquor for clean fruit expression, sake for mellowness, brandy for complexity), your preferred sweetness level, and serving style. Many Japanese liqueurs are best on the rocks or as a soda highball.
